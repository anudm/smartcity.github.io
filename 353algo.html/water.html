Business Case: Water Management System in Flood Scenarios

Sub-task: Disaster Resilience and Water Management

SDG Goal: 11

Target: 11.B

Indicator: 11.B.1

Effective water management is essential to increase disaster resilience, especially in flood-prone areas. The implementation of water management systems supports SDG Goal 11 by mitigating flood risks and improving the resilience of urban infrastructure. Below are the specific targets and indicators:

  • Target 11.B: Increase the number of cities adopting disaster resilience strategies for water management.
  • Indicator 11.B.1: Proportion of cities with comprehensive flood management and water risk reduction plans.

Water Management System Applications

  • 1. Arrays:Used to store historical water levels for reservoirs and rivers for quick access and computation.
  • 2. Structures:Define water resources with properties like capacity, flow rate, and quality.
    • struct WaterResource {
    int id;
    float capacity;
    float currentLevel;
    float quality;
};
  • 3. List:Represent connected components of a water distribution network.
  • 4. Stack:Used in depth-first search (DFS) to detect cycles in the water pipeline network.
  • 5. Trie:Manage hierarchical names of water resource locations.
  • 6. Heap :Manage priority queues for flood alerts and evacuation planning.
  • 7. Lookup Table :Direct access to water quality data based on region.
  • 8. Fenwick Tree :Perform range updates and queries on cumulative rainfall data.
