Business Case: Water Management System in Flood Scenarios

Sub-task: Disaster Resilience and Water Management

SDG Goal: 11

Target: 11.B

Indicator: 11.B.1

Effective water management is essential to increase disaster resilience, especially in flood-prone areas. The implementation of water management systems supports SDG Goal 11 by mitigating flood risks and improving the resilience of urban infrastructure. Below are the specific targets and indicators:

  • Target 11.B: Increase the number of cities adopting disaster resilience strategies for water management.
  • Indicator 11.B.1: Proportion of cities with comprehensive flood management and water risk reduction plans.

The following water management facilities are monitored to ensure effective flood control and water risk reduction:

Water Management System Applications

Water Management System Applications

  • 1. Arrays

    Used to store historical water levels for reservoirs and rivers for quick access and computation.

  • 2. Structures

    Define water resources with properties like capacity, flow rate, and quality.

    struct WaterResource {
    int id;
    float capacity;
    float currentLevel;
    float quality;
};
  • 3. List

    Represent connected components of a water distribution network.

  • 4. Stack

    Used in depth-first search (DFS) to detect cycles in the water pipeline network.

  • 5. Trie

    Manage hierarchical names of water resource locations.

    • 6. Heap

    Manage priority queues for flood alerts and evacuation planning.

  • 7. Lookup Table<

    Direct access to water quality data based on region.

  • 8. Fenwick Tree

    Perform range updates and queries on cumulative rainfall data.

  • 9. Union-Find

    Identify connected components of water pipelines.

  • 10. Hashing

    Quickly retrieve water quality reports based on region IDs.

  • 11. Depth First Search (DFS)

    Detect pipeline cycles and find disconnected regions.

  • 12. Breadth First Search (BFS)

    Find the shortest path for water delivery in a city.

  • 13. Bubble Sort

    Sort pipeline maintenance schedules by urgency.

  • 14. Selection Sort

    Sort reservoirs by descending capacity for flood planning.

  • 15. Insertion Sort

    Insert newly reported flood regions into the sorted list.

  • 16. Rabin-Karp

    Match region names in large datasets for consistency checks.

  • 17. Knuth-Morris-Pratt

    Search patterns in rainfall data logs.

  • 18. Heap Sort

    Sort flood zones by priority for evacuation.

  • 19. Kruskal

    Build minimum-cost water pipeline connections.

  • 20. Prim

    Design an optimal water distribution network.

  • 21. Floyd-Warshall

    Find all-pairs shortest paths for water distribution.

  • 22.Bellman-Ford

    Handle networks with negative weights (e.g., cost savings).
