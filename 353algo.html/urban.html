Steps and Applications

1. Optimizing Green Space Connectivity

Using Kruskal’s Algorithm, planners developed a Minimum Spanning Tree that connects 10 green spaces with minimal road construction costs.

Kruskal’s Algorithm

Kruskal’s algorithm is a Greedy algorithm used for finding the minimum spanning tree of a graph. It sorts all the edges in increasing order of their weights and adds them one by one to the spanning tree, provided they do not form a cycle. To view the code of kruskal's click here

Key points:

  • Sorts the edges and adds them in sequence if no cycles are formed.
  • It is used for finding the Minimum Spanning Tree (MST) of a graph.
  • Falls under the Greedy technique.
  • With an efficient union-find algorithm, the time complexity is dominated by the sorting step, resulting in an overall time complexity of O(|E| log |E|), where E is the number of edges.

3. Prioritizing Development Projects

Projects were ranked based on urgency and impact using Merge Sort. Top projects include:

  • Converting unused land into parks
  • Upgrading roads to reduce travel times

Merge Sort

Merge Sort is a divide and conquer algorithm for sorting an array. It divides the array into two halves, recursively sorts them, and then merges the sorted halves to produce the sorted array.
