SDG Contributions

1. Healthcare

Healthcare supports SDG 3: Good Health and Well-being by ensuring access to essential health services, reducing financial hardships, and improving overall quality of life.

Target 3.8

Achieve universal health coverage (UHC), including financial risk protection, access to quality healthcare services, and affordable essential medicines.

UHC ensures that no one is left behind in accessing critical healthcare services, regardless of their socioeconomic status.

Indicator 3.8.2

Proportion of population with large household expenditures on health as a share of total household expenditure or income.

This measures financial hardship due to healthcare costs, emphasizing the need for affordable healthcare systems worldwide.

2. Transportation

Transportation supports SDG 11: Sustainable Cities and Communities by fostering accessible, safe, and efficient transport systems that reduce urban congestion and improve connectivity.

Target 11.2

Provide access to safe, affordable, accessible, and sustainable transport systems for all, with special attention to the needs of vulnerable groups.

Inclusive transport systems enhance mobility for people with disabilities, the elderly, and those in underserved areas.

Indicator 11.2.1

Proportion of population with convenient access to public transport.

This ensures that urban and rural communities benefit from reliable, affordable, and efficient public transit networks.

3. Education

Education supports SDG 4: Quality Education by promoting lifelong learning opportunities and improving literacy and numeracy for all.

Target 4.6

Ensure that all youth and a substantial proportion of adults achieve literacy and numeracy by 2030.

Improved literacy and numeracy empower individuals to actively participate in the economy and contribute to societal development.

Indicator 4.6.1

Percentage of the population achieving at least a fixed level of proficiency in functional literacy and numeracy.

This reflects the effectiveness of educational systems in building foundational skills necessary for lifelong learning.
