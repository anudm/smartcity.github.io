City Plan for Transportation - SDG Goal 11 Case Study

Total City Area: 1500 sq km

Transportation Infrastructure Area: 400 sq km

SDG Goal 11: Sustainable Cities and Communities

SDG Goal: 11

Target 11.2: Provide access to safe, affordable, accessible, and sustainable transport systems for all.

Indicator 11.2.1: Proportion of the population that has convenient access to public transport, by sex, age, and persons with disabilities.

Transportation Planning Overview

Category Details
Mass Transit Systems Implementation of metro, bus rapid transit (BRT), and light rail systems across urban areas.
Smart Traffic Management Real-time traffic monitoring and control systems to reduce congestion.
EV Charging Stations Deployment of electric vehicle charging points in high-density areas.
Bike and Pedestrian Lanes Dedicated lanes for non-motorized transport to promote sustainability.
Integrated Transportation Hubs Seamless connections between different modes of transportation.

Challenges and Consequences:

  • Traffic Congestion: Increased vehicle density leading to delays and inefficiencies.
  • Energy Consumption: High energy demands for electric transit systems.
  • Infrastructure Scalability: The need to accommodate future population growth and demand.

Solutions and Algorithms:

1. Dijkstra's Algorithm – Solution:

For route optimization, Dijkstra's Algorithm identifies the shortest paths between transportation nodes, ensuring efficient movement of people and goods. It is applied to design effective transit routes for buses and metro systems, reducing travel time and congestion.

Dijkstra Algorithm

2. Kruskal's Algorithm – Solution:

To build a cost-effective and efficient transportation network, Kruskal's Algorithm is used to construct minimum spanning trees. It helps in connecting key transit hubs with minimal construction costs while ensuring optimal connectivity.

Kruskal Algorithm

3. Floyd-Warshall Algorithm – Solution:

For analyzing all-pairs shortest paths in the city's transit network, Floyd-Warshall Algorithm is implemented. This ensures that every area within the city is well-connected and accessible, particularly for emergency and healthcare transportation.

Floyd-Warshall Algorithm

Key Focus Areas:

  • Urban Planning: Designing a sustainable and integrated transportation network.
  • Energy Efficiency: Utilizing renewable energy sources for EV charging stations.
  • Accessibility: Ensuring all citizens, including persons with disabilities, have access to transport.
  • Scalability: Preparing for future growth with scalable transit solutions.

This case study demonstrates how transportation planning aligns with SDG Goal 11, focusing on accessibility, efficiency, and sustainability.
